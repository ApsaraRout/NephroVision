Why Check eGFR?

  • Early kidney damage has no symptoms
  • Protects from heart disease & stroke
  • Very important if you have:
    • Diabetes
    • High blood pressure
    • Family history of kidney disease
  • Recommended for everyone above 50–55 years
  • Simple blood test → powerful insight

Rising Global Burden of CKD

  • Prevalent cases nearly doubled: 351M (1990) → 674M
  • Deaths increasing significantly worldwide
  • Main drivers: diabetes, hypertension, obesity
  • Projections: CKD deaths may double by 2040

Source: Global Burden of Disease Study

eGFR Stages (KDIGO)

  • ≥ 90 → G1 Normal or high
  • 60–89 → G2 Mildly decreased
  • 45–59 → G3a Mild–moderate
  • 30–44 → G3b Moderate–severe
  • 15–29 → G4 Severe
  • < 15 → G5 Kidney failure

Lower eGFR + protein in urine = higher risk

Common Causes of CKD

Diabetes and hypertension together are responsible for approximately 75% of kidney failure cases worldwide.
Diabetes remains the leading cause, contributing to 40–50% of chronic kidney disease.
Controlling blood sugar and blood pressure early can significantly slow or prevent CKD progression.
